I am writing to you regarding your 2011 *Nuclear Fusion* paper on the high-temperature FRC merging and compression experiments performed at the IPA device.

Our system ranges from offline full-fidelity solvers (Grad-Shafranov/Rust) to ultra-fast 10 MHz stochastic neural networks designed for RMF phase-locking and active instability suppression.

We are in the process of validating our non-adiabatic pulsed compression models (FUS-C.6) and our 1D rigid-rotor oracles. Specifically, we have selected the results from **Shot 2001 (Figure 5)** as our primary validation target for experimental parity.

While we have successfully reconstructed a trajectory based on the anchor points and timescales reported in the paper, we are aiming for full-fidelity validation to satisfy our internal safety and performance contracts. Would you be willing to share the raw digitized time-series data (B_ext, R_s, T_total, and n_e) for Shot 2001?

In the spirit of collaborative open science, we would be very happy to share our results, our neuro-symbolic methodology, and the benchmarking artifacts generated by coupling our control kernels with your experimental dataset. We believe these results could provide interesting insights into the real-time controllability of such high-performance FRC states.

Thank you for your time and for your pioneering work in this field.
