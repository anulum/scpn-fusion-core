Make the PB-KBM saturate-branch test robust across Python versions

`test_full_second_stability_can_saturate_the_scan` exercises the "entire scan
stable" branch (the prediction saturates at T_max instead of crossing a
peeling-ballooning boundary). Its original parameters (Ip=5 MA, T_max=1.0) put
the pedestal right on the convergence boundary: the peeling term j/j_crit reached
~0.988, so the pb-boundary radius sat at ~0.988 on the CI Python 3.12 runner
(whole scan stable → saturate) but tipped just over 1 on Python 3.10/3.11, where a
sub-percent floating-point difference in the Sauter bootstrap current flips the
scan to a found crossing (converged=True). The test then failed on 3.10/3.11.

Re-anchor the case with a wide stability margin: Ip=15 MA (much larger
peeling-current limit) and T_max=0.5 keV give a pb-boundary radius of ~0.13 —
comfortably below 1 — so the whole KBM family is stable by a margin no
cross-version numerical difference can cross. The test still covers the same
"first_unstable is None" saturate branch (eped_pb_kbm.py stays at 100% line
coverage) and now additionally asserts the radius is well below 1 to lock in the
margin. Lesson: never assert a branch selection that hinges on a value sitting at
a numerical boundary — pick inputs with headroom.
